Even though hardly anyone will take the time to keep up with this, I'm still not sure how much I should say about what it is I'm building. To put it in a sentence:

*Tera* (name TBD) will be like Strava but for traveling: A better way to make travel plans, log memories, and stay connected with friends and family while exploring the world. 

Right now, I want to make a functioning app by the end of February (this could be a very naive estimate). However, I think laying down a deadline like this for myself should be very beneficial in my progress, regardless if I make the deadline or not. 

During this first month of January, while I was still getting back into the flow of things in San Diego, I definitely learned a lot about myself in terms of how I work (TLDR: I tend to avoid it at all costs). While the need for structure (deadline) is definitely part of it, it was also enlightening since this is the first time I've proverbially been the captain of my own ship. And almost immediately, I lost sight of what it is I actually wanted to do with this project.

That's why, going forward, I'm working under one rule: I want to make a product that I actually **want to use**. While it would be nice for Tera to be flashy or popular or successful, I got lost worrying about how to make it that and did less work as a result. While I tend to be my own worst enemy, I hope the hope of building something for myself will make me less reluctant to grind than I usually am. 

I think I'll end these posts off with a completely subjective progress metric. This month, while I learned about the standard app production workflow, lay out the bare skeleton of an app, and did some tests with location tracking, Tera is still missing almost every core feature AND I ran into some mysterious bugs with the app startup and the database, so I'm assigning it a 5% so far 😵‍💫
